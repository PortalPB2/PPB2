About The Mod
0/3

Project Beta² is a pair of modifications to the original Portal which takes fans back to the early previews of the game, allowing them to experience what it was like to play durng the beta-stage of development. The mod aims to be as accurate as possible per-demo based on published footage from the time. Each demo contains correct portal and portal gun textures, as well as the male character model which was used before Chell was included as the protagonist of the game.

To install the mod, download the zip using the button below. Ensure Portal is already installed (not Portal RTX), then extract the mod folders from the zip and drag them into your sourcemods folder. Restart steam and the mod should show up in your library.

02
Leipzig Demo
2/3

The Leipzig demo is based on the recorded demo shown at the 2006 Games Convention in Leipzig, Germany. This version has the "alpha" portals with an animated mask and overlay. The chambers appear darker than the retail version due to lighting setting changes and differences in the way the engine and tools calculated the lighting. Turrets behave differently - they don't move after firing due to being knocked over, and they can be subsequently reactivated.

Overall, signage around the levels is more simplistic. The indicator lines around the stages are solid orange lines, and they lead to green/red door indicator boxes. The security camera models are generic camera models instead of the aperture-ized versions from the retail build. When picking up objects with the portal gun, an orange light shines and a prominent effect is played at the muzzle of the gun, similar to the HL2 gravity gun. In addition, light rail platforms use an older sound effect.

03
Barcelona Demo
3/3

The Barcelona demo is based on the demo from Microsoft's 2006 X06 Conference in Barcelona, Spain. Portals in this demo were redesigned to have a spiral appearance, as the original "alpha" portals took up way too much memory on the original Xbox.

The crosshair now changes to orange when an item is picked up. The test chambers have a brighter appearance. The indicator lines are now orange dotted-lines. Certain sounds, like button sounds, are the same as they appear in retail builds.
